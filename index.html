Biography

I am a PhD student at University of Texas at Austin, in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering. I work at the Laboratory for Image and Video Engineering (LIVE) with Prof. Alan Bovik as a Graduate Research Assistant. We use statistical, signal processing and machine learning methods to evaluate and optimize the subjective quality of images and videos. This is especially relevant in the context of the online video explosion in the recent years.

I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology, Hyderabad with a Bachelors in Electrical Engineering, where I was awared an Institute Silver Medal for having the highest score in my department. While at IITH, I worked with Dr. Sumohana Channappayya on applying Deep Learning to Fourier Ptychography. During the summer of 2018, I interned at Carnegie Mellon University as a research intern with Prof. Katia Sycara under the SN Bose Scholar Program where I worked on developing a biologically-inspired safe Reinforcement Learning algorithm. I was also a summer intern at Uurmi Systems (C/o Mathworks Inc.) where I worked on optimizing stereo matching algorithms for GPUs using CUDA.

When I am not trying to figure out why you didn’t like that one cat video as much as the others, I play badminton for leisure and write amateur poetry.
